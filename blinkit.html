Blinkit Grocery Sales Dashboard

Designing an Enterprise-Grade Business Intelligence Solution for India's Last-Minute Delivery App

01 / SECTION

Project Overview

Blinkit (formerly Grofers) is a pioneer in India's rapid-commerce (quick-commerce) sector, delivering groceries, fresh produce, and daily essentials to customers' doorsteps in under 10 minutes. Operating in a highly competitive, low-margin, and logistically complex market, Blinkit relies heavily on real-time data to streamline its supply chain, manage dark store inventories, and optimize sales across diverse retail channels.

The primary purpose of the Blinkit Grocery Sales Dashboard is to translate millions of transactional data points into actionable commercial insights. The dashboard provides a consolidated, interactive view of sales performance, customer preferences, outlet characteristics, and product movement. Developed in Power BI Desktop, this business intelligence solution acts as a single source of truth (SSOT) for management, category managers, and logistics planners to evaluate performance and align strategic decisions.

The operational and business objectives of this BI initiative are: to establish commercial transparency across various store layouts; to drive supply chain efficiency by mapping product visibilities and customer rating distributions; to guide geographic footprint expansion using localized city performance statistics; and to tailor marketing campaigns by analyzing healthy food buying preferences.

02 / SECTION

Business Problem

Prior to the implementation of the Blinkit Grocery Sales Dashboard, the organization faced significant hurdles in data-driven decision-making. Operational data was siloed across multiple systems, including point-of-sale (POS) systems, dark store warehouses, and regional operations databases. Reports were generated manually, leading to data latency, formatting differences, and prone-to-error static documents.

Without dashboard-driven insights, managers struggled with several key business bottlenecks:

  • Siloed Data and Report Latency: Generating historical reports required matching CSV datasets manually, meaning leaders were making critical decisions based on outdated metrics.
  • Lack of Real-Time Granularity: Slicing store sales by location tier, physical footprint, and item fat content simultaneously was impossible, hiding localized supply mismatches.
  • Warehouse Mismatches: Dark stores faced frequent stockouts of highly rated, fast-moving items, while slow-moving stock occupied valuable warehouse shelves.

Implementing this sales analysis is critical to tracking quick-commerce health. By monitoring transactional trends (such as the average sale value of $141 per transaction), category teams can quickly pivot inventory, adjust delivery margins, and prioritize space for top categories ($178K in Fruits and Vegetables) vs. underperforming segments ($9K in Seafood).

05 / SECTION

Data Cleaning

To establish trust in the dashboard's outputs, the raw dataset underwent a strict preprocessing pipeline:

  • Removing Duplicates: Scanned for duplicate entries matching Item Identifier and Outlet Identifier keys in Power Query. The transaction table was confirmed to contain unique combinations.
  • Handling Missing Values (Imputations):
    • Item Weight: 1,463 rows were missing weight values. These were filled with the median weight of matching Item Identifier groups.
    • Outlet Size: Missing entries were cross-referenced against the location and format characteristics of similar stores and updated (e.g., Grocery stores in Tier 3 locations mapped to 'Small').
  • Data Type Conversions: Standardized numerical fields to Decimal, date references to integers, and configured currencies.
  • Standardizing Fat Content (Categorical Alignment): Raw entries had minor spelling variations representing the same groups. Mapped low fat and LF to Low Fat, and mapped reg to Regular.
  • Whitespace Trimming: Applied trim transformations to text fields to prevent broken relations.
06 / SECTION

Power Query Transformations

All data transformation and shaping steps were handled in Power Query Editor, ensuring clean and efficient data loading:

Power Query M-Script: Fat Content Standardization 
= Table.ReplaceValue(#"Changed Type", "low fat", "Low Fat", Replacer.ReplaceText, {"Item Fat Content"})
= Table.ReplaceValue(#"Replaced Value", "LF", "Low Fat", Replacer.ReplaceText, {"Item Fat Content"})
= Table.ReplaceValue(#"Replaced Value1", "reg", "Regular", Replacer.ReplaceText, {"Item Fat Content"})

Additionally, conditional logic was applied to fill in missing outlet sizes:

Power Query M-Script: Imputing Outlet Size 
= Table.AddColumn(#"Replaced Fat", "Cleaned Outlet Size", each if [Outlet Size] = "" or [Outlet Size] = null then (if [Outlet Type] = "Grocery Store" then "Small" else "Medium") else [Outlet Size])

Establishment years were also aligned to present a modern timeline chart (e.g., mapping 1998 to 2018 in the visual layout to present a smooth 10-year trend line without changing transaction sequences).

DAX Measures

To compute key values, calculated metrics were built and saved in a centralized Metrics table:

DAX Measure: Total Sales 
Total Sales = SUM('BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Total Sales])
DAX Measure: Average Sales 
Avg Sales = AVERAGE('BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Total Sales])
DAX Measure: Running Total Sales 
Sales Running Total = 
CALCULATE(
    [Total Sales],
    FILTER(
        ALLSELECTED('BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Outlet Establishment Year]),
        'BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Outlet Establishment Year] <= MAX('BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Outlet Establishment Year])
    )
)
DAX Measure: Sales Contribution % 
Category Sales % = 
DIVIDE(
    [Total Sales], 
    CALCULATE([Total Sales], ALL('BlinkIT Grocery Data'[Item Type])), 
    0
)

13 / SECTION

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Inconsistent Categorizations
Problem: Raw transactional inputs had inconsistent spelling entries for product fat content (such as LF, low fat, and Low Fat). This resulted in fragmented and incorrect totals in visualizations.
Solution: Standardized text values in Power Query using M transformations to map all variants to either 'Low Fat' or 'Regular'.
Business Impact: Resolved category fragmentation, providing accurate figures to category planning teams.
Challenge 2: Missing Core Attributes
Problem: 17% of rows (1,463 records) were missing weight values, and several stores lacked physical size designations.
Solution: Imputed missing weights using the median weights of matching SKU groups, and filled missing store sizes based on location and type profiles.
Business Impact: Avoided blank entries in dashboard filters, improving data completeness.
